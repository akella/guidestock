How it works

You know what it’s like: You travel to some interesting site and are so fascinated with what you see that you can’t help but share your impressions with others. That’s exactly what we encourage you to do at Guidestock.

Imagine yourself sitting in a café with your friends, telling them about your incredible trip. Guidestock is just the same; only we’re going to pay you for your personal impressions.

When you create a guide, you can combine all the different points of interest you’d recommend visiting. Even just one single place, if you feel it merits that kind of attention – but keep in mind that the richer and more informative your guide is, the more popular it will be among travellers.

Feel free to experiment with our toolkit and create a unique “trip salad” recipe adding any ingredients you like!

How to create a guide

  • 1

    Use a map to mark and name checkpoint(s) along your route. Make sure the distance between two checkpoints is short enough that a person can comfortably walk from one to the other.

  • 2

    While you work, the toolkit shows how long it will take a traveler to get from point to point.

  • Drag and drop any recordings you may have already created, or import them from your internal library, then add music or anything else you believe might fit and captivate the listener.

  • Or use the recording tool to create brand new recordings. Make sure your narration matches what a person will see while walking between the checkpoints.

  • If your story refers to specific features about a place or building that the traveler should pay attention to while they are being described, select that recording and click on “Stand” mode.

  • 3

    Create a short and entertaining title for your guide that describes the contents clearly.

  • 4

    Select the language you used to record your guide.

  • 5

    Add a description to your guide that would make it more personal; this is your chance to persuade others to follow your guide.

When you’re done, click Save if you want to keep your changes for now, and reserve the chance
to edit the guide later; or Submit if the guide is complete and ready to be reviewed.
